# Naan

*Recipe by Pooja Makhijani, via King Arthur Baking — [kingarthurbaking.com](https://www.kingarthurbaking.com/recipes/naan-recipe)*

*Soft, chewy, slightly tangy flatbread cooked in a cast iron pan — no tandoor required.*

---

## Details

- **Prep time:** 30 minutes
- **Rise time:** 50–60 minutes + 20 minutes rest
- **Cook time:** 15–20 minutes total (cooking 8 naans in batches)
- **Yield:** 8 flatbreads

---

## Ingredients

### Dough
- 180g (1½ cups) all-purpose flour
- 90g (¾ cup) bread flour
- 142g (½ cup + 2 tbsp) warm water
- 71g (5 tbsp) full-fat plain Greek yogurt
- 28g (2 tbsp) ghee or butter, melted
- 1½ tsp instant yeast
- 1 tsp granulated sugar
- 1 tsp table salt

### Topping
- 28g (2 tbsp) ghee or butter, melted
- 2 tsp nigella seeds *(optional)*
- ¼ cup fresh cilantro, roughly chopped *(optional)*

---

## Instructions

### 1. Make the Dough
Combine all dough ingredients in a large bowl (or stand mixer). Mix until shaggy, then knead until the dough is smooth, bouncy, and slightly tacky.

### 2. First Rise
Place dough in a greased bowl, cover, and let rise until doubled — about **50–60 minutes**.

### 3. Divide and Rest
Divide dough into 8 equal pieces (about 65g each). Shape each into a ball, cover, and rest for **20 minutes**.

### 4. Preheat the Pan
While the dough rests, preheat a cast iron pan over medium-high heat (or an electric griddle to medium-high).

### 5. Shape
On a lightly floured surface, hand-stretch or roll each ball into a 6" round or 7" oblong for thicker naan, or 8"/10" for thinner. Keep remaining pieces covered.

### 6. Cook
Cook one naan at a time. Place in the hot pan and cook **30–40 seconds**, until bubbles form on top and the underside browns. Flip and cook another **40–50 seconds** until done.

### 7. Finish and Serve
Brush immediately with melted ghee or butter. Sprinkle with nigella seeds and cilantro if desired. Keep finished naan warm in a 200°F oven or wrapped in foil until serving.

---

## Storage
Well-wrapped at room temperature for 2 days. Freeze for longer storage.

---

## Variations

- **Garlic naan:** Mince 5–6 garlic cloves. Press into each rolled-out round before cooking.
- **Stuffed naan:** Roll each ball into a 5" round, place 2 tbsp filling in the center (spiced mashed potato, grated cheese, or paneer work well), pinch edges together to seal, then roll out to 6"–8" before cooking.

---

## Tips

- **Use your hands to shape, not a rolling pin.** A rolling pin pushes out the gas and leads to flat, dense naan.
- **Bread flour is key.** It gives the naan its signature chew and makes the dough easier to stretch without tearing.
- **Watch for bubbles.** That's your cue to flip — when small bubbles appear all over the surface, the first side is ready.
- **Ghee > butter** if you can find it. It adds a nutty richness that's hard to replicate.
